Changunarayan Temple

Changunarayan Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Nepal, is a revered temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, known for its exquisite architecture and intricate stone carvings, offering panoramic views and spiritual devotion.

Detail about Changunarayan

Changunarayan

Changu Narayan, a two-story pagoda-style temple in Nepal, is the oldest Hindu temple in the Kathmandu Valley, dating back to the fourth century. The temple, built on a high hill, features intricate carvings of the 10 incarnations of Vishnu and various Tantric goddesses. The main temple is decorated with the finest carvings in the Kathmandu Valley, while the surrounding statues are the best outside the National Museum of Nepal. The oldest statues date back to the Licchavi era (400 to 750), featuring Garuda, the legendary bird that served as Vishnu's mount. The temple complex has survived several threats, including a fire in 1702 and the Gorkha Earthquake of 2015, which damaged 753 temples, shrines, and monasteries across Nepal. Despite the damage, the temple was reopened in November 2017 and restoration projects began, despite visitor numbers declining following the earthquake.

Things To Do In Changunarayan

Nepal Yoga Academy.

Nepal Yoga Academy is a renowned center for yoga and spiritual education, offering seminars, workshops, and retreats to enhance instruction, research, and practice based on scientific yogic ideas, fostering a supportive atmosphere for students.

Changu Museum

The Changunarayan Museum in Nepal houses a diverse collection of antiques, coins, utensils, and implements, showcasing Nepal's cultural heritage and everyday life. It attracts visitors and enthusiasts interested in learning about the region's history.

SunapatiThanka Painting School

Sunapati Thangka Painting School in Nepal promotes and preserves traditional Thangka painting, a significant art form in Tibetan Buddhist culture. It offers comprehensive courses and workshops, attracting local and international enthusiasts.
