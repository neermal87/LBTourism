Manakamana Temple

Manakamana Temple, known as the "Temple of Wishes," is a popular destination in Nepal, known for its serene atmosphere, spiritual significance, and stunning natural beauty, often visited during festivals.

Detail About Manakamana

Manakamana

The Manakamana Temple is located 1,300 metres (4,300 ft) above sea level on the Kafakdada Hill which sits in the confluence between Trishuli and Marsyangdi in the Sahid Lakhan Rural Municipality in Gorkha, Gandaki Province, Nepal.It is approximately 106 kilometres (66 mi) west of Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal, and about 94 kilometres (58 mi) east of Pokhara. Many mountains can be seen from the hill including Annapurna II, Lamjung Himal, and Baudha which is part of Manaslu, the eighth-highest mountain in the world. By hiking from Anbu Khaireni Rural Municipality it takes about three hours to reach Manakamana which is about 1,000 metres (3,300 ft) away. Alternatively, pilgrims can take the Manakamana Cable Car which was built in 1998 for about US$7.5 million.

Things To Do In Manakamana

Visit Manakamana Temple.

Manakāmanā Mandira, lit 'temple that grants wishes of its devotees') is a Hindu temple dedicated to goddess Bhagwati, an incarnation of Parvati and it is situated in the village of Manakamana in Gorkha District, Gandaki Province, Nepal founded by Arman Rayamajhi in 1678.

Cable Car Ride

One of the highlights of the pilgrimage to Manakamana Temple is the thrilling cable car ride that transports visitors from Kurintar to the hilltop where the temple is situated. Here's a glimpse into the experience of embarking on this breathtaking journey.

Hiking and Trekking

A renowned destination of choice for avid hiking enthusiasts for its wide selection and captivating routes. Among these are the Aanboo Khaireni route, the Kuringhat via Taklung route, and the iconic Gorkha Temple route. These trails feature a view of the majestic mountain ranges, lush jungles, and picturesque suspension bridges, leaving an unforgettable impression. Hikers can also venture through the Shahid Lakhan Gaunpalika VDC, with the chance to fully immerse in the vibrant local culture and enjoy a wide variety of traditional culinary delights.
